Organism
Live — Magara Jam 2025

A 2D endless tower defense built in 72 hours by a team of three. The premise is deceptively simple: you control a living, procedurally growing organism — rotating it to shield against incoming projectiles and hold off escalating enemy waves for as long as possible. The longer you survive, the more your organism grows, and the more complex the juggle becomes.

Boss encounters punctuate the endless loop, forcing the player to adapt before the next wave of pressure hits. The central question the game keeps asking is the same one it opens with — how long can you preserve the integrity of your organism?

In Development

A 3D first-person endless roguelite built around relentless momentum — inspired by the chaotic energy of Megabonk and Ultrakill. Pick a character, chain skills, and tear through escalating enemy waves. Kills feed an XP pool, levels unlock power, and a boss gates every map before the next one opens. The loop is tight and endless by design.

Under the surface, the game runs on a stack of deliberate Unity systems: a global object pool drives every XP orb, enemy, and projectile with zero allocation overhead, and GPU-baked animations cycle enemy meshes through looped frames — keeping draw calls low while dozens of entities flood the screen simultaneously.

2 months was spent on the development process.
